Here are my Top 5 Los Angeles Eateries

Janeane Kim


As someone living in a city far too crowded for it's own good, here are a list of 5 of my favorite places to eat when I forget about my bank balance every once in awhile.

Featured joints:

  1. The Boiling Crab
  2. Pampas Grill
  3. Sugarfish by Nozawa (の(No)ざ(za)わ(wa))
  4. In N' Out
  5. My Kitchen

The Breakdown:

Place Price Quality
The Boiling Crab $$ 3.5/5
Pampas Grill $$ 3.5/5
Sugarfish $$$ 5/5
In N' Out $ 4/5
My Kitchen $ 1/5 (I can't cook)

1. The Boiling Crab


The Boiling Crab is a great place for those looking to get their seafood fix and open up their own seafood like one of those achaeology dig kits we all used as a kid. Full of salt and spice and a huge selection, you can't go wrong.

    Pros

  • Great hands on activity
  • Customizable
  • Large selection

    • Cons

  • Price varies (by market rate)
  • Long wait during dinner
  • Not for those with hypernatremia

2. Pampas Grill


Pampas Grill located in the heart of the Farmers' Market at the Grove is a great place for shoppers, foodies, and tourists alike. It's perfect for groups: don't like Brazilian? Choose from the 20+ other food stalls open right next door!

    Pros

  • Prime (rib) location
  • Delicious meats
  • Get to fix your own plate

    • Cons

  • Outdoor seating only
  • Long lines
  • By-weight payment

3. Sugarfish by Nozawa(の(No)ざ(za)わ(wa))


Sugarfish is where you want to go for quality sushi, and good atmosphere. Although a bit pricy for my college student unemployed wallet, it's a great place to go if you want to treat yourself or a special someone.

    Pros

  • Great mood lights
  • Accessible delivery system
  • Simple menu

    • Cons

  • Not customizable
  • Take-what-they-give-you seating
  • Your wallet will protest

4. In N' Out


The legend itself. What more is there to ask for besides steaming crunchy, fries, greasy, warm buns, and a wonderfully juicy bun? Cheap, delicious, and will never ever let you down so long as you're willing to wait in line.

    Pros

  • Many locations
  • Simple menu with many customizations
  • Milkshakes

    • Cons

  • Long lines
  • Messy

5. My Kitchen


This is where I spend most my meals. Gets the job done (but at what cost?) This is not my kitchen by the way...

    Pros

  • I feel like a cooking channel Youtuber everyday
  • No travel/delivery fees
  • Customizable to the max

    • Cons

  • Depression when the fridge is empty
  • My mom will yell at me for using all the onions
  • Cleanup
